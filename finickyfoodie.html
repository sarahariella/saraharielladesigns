Finicky Foodie App Design

User Experience Case Study

The Project

The goal of the project was to design an app where users are presented with recipes that exclude foods/ingredients that they dislike. A "suggested recipes" feature also needed to be included in our app. We had to come up with a way the app would be able to determine and filter out disliked foods of the user, and provide an excellent user experience as well. We were responsible for coming up with our own target market, as well as adding a feature that would make our app unique from other apps already on the market.

Project Brief

Overview

Picky eaters unite! Finicky Foodie is an app designed to make cooking enjoyable for everyone. Whether you have an allergy, follow a diet that restricts ingredients, or maybe just don’t like mushrooms, Finicky Foodie is the app for you. By creating a personalized and customizable experience, every Finicky Foodie account is unique to the user making the app feel personal. Filtering out ingredients, suggested recipes and even a grocery shopping list are just some of the features the app has to offer. Bon Apetit!

Problem

We must consider that many people are not only cooking for themselves. It may be a roomate, sibling or significant other in which case sometimes trying new things might be something fun. However, with kids, it’s a whole different story. Having to come up with new recipes that your kids will actually want to eat can be exhausting, especially after a long day at work. Kids can be huge picky eaters, which means getting them to try new things seems like an impossible task to accomplish.

Solution

After coming home from a long day at work, any user can feel confidant turning to the Finicky Foodie app for dinner ideas. Recipes are customized for allergies, dietary restrictions and picky eaters especially. The Kid-Friendly approach to the app allows for less complex recipes that are easy for any parent to follow, and for every kid to enjoy! By taking ingredients someone likes and filtering out ingredients someone doesn’t like, the result is a customized cookbook that will lead to trying new meals, but most importantly, making memories with food.

Approach

In order to successfully develop this app, one must dive deep into understanding the user persona. What is it like in the day of the user? When will they realistically be using the app? How often? How would using Finicky Foodie make their lives easier? Most importantly, how can we ensure that the user is going to continue using the app, not just download and delete? We’ll have to take a look at the competition to see what some apps are doing right, and what others are doing wrong. Looking at reviews can also help us understand why users get frustrated using recipe apps, and what gets them to become loyal users, which essentially is the end goal of Finicky Foodie. By using a sophisticated and colorful design, we want to make users happy, and keep them coming back for more.

"The app should feel like a custom cookbook for each family."

Competitve Analysis

A competitve analysis was conducted on other food apps with the same target market as Finicky Foodie. We are able to analyze different features, strengths, and weaknesses to see what would be beneficial to our own app. Analyzing the user experience of each feature allows us to focus on what a user would want to experience and wouldn’t want to experience with the Finicky Foodie app. An easy to use app with lots of customizabily, as well as easy to follow recipes with lots of visuals are the keys to success to ensure a great user experience

Strengths and Weaknesses

Tasty

Strengths

  • Recipe experience is great, video clips to follow along
  • App is really easy to use and navigate

Weaknesses

  • Customizability options are dissapointing

Yummly

Strengths

  • Amazing customizability
  • App is easy to use and navigate
  • Sign-up process was unique

Weaknesses

  • Recipes are links to other sites
  • Can only search by ingredient

Mealime

Strengths

  • Personalized meal plans
  • Organized shopping list
  • Good customizability

Weaknesses

  • Need to upgrade to pro version to access many features
  • Users reviews found a lot of recipes quite difficult

My Fridge Food

Strengths

  • Takes ingredients from fridge to create recipes
  • Some users enjoy a simple app that is to the point with minimal features

Weaknesses

  • Customizability options are very limited
  • App is confusing to navigate
  • No log-in/account feature

My Unique Feature

The unique feature that I came up with I decided to call "Recipe Magic". After researching other recipe apps, I noticed that they were all catered to the individual. I wanted to come up with something that would also be able to work in a group, specifically family, environment.

The feature allows the main user of the account to select who is eating, and will then generate recipes based on the allergies, dietary restrictions, and dislikes of every user selected. I thought that this would be perfect for families with children. If one child has a peanut allergy, and another child dislikes onions, for example, the generated recipes will exclude both peanuts and onions, or offer substitute ingredient suggestions. This refers back to the solution of the project: creating a custom cookbook for every family.

Reflection

What I learned from this project is just how important it is to really understand the target user. One must really consider what features would truly benefit the user and make their experience a positive one. I believe a key part to this case study was the user persona research and competitve analysis. In the user persona research, it was really important to empathize with the user; to put yourself in their shoes. What is 'a day in the life' of the user? When would they be using your app? What makes the app an ideal solution to the problem the user faces? It also helps us determine which features are the most essential to the user. Figuring out what features are the most important and impactful to the user versus features that may not as essential. It gives us a very good starting point of what to focus on and taught me a lot about understanding your target user.

During the competitive analysis, I learned about how important it is to explore similar products (in this case other mobile applications) that are out on the market. Conducting research on these apps, downloading them, and testing out their user experiences really helped me to determine what I wanted to include in my own app design. For example, by reading user reviews of apps on the App Store, I was able to understand why users enjoyed one app over another, and what features were important to users. For example, having customizability features was appreciated by users with dietary restrictions, which made the decision for me to include dietary customizability essential.

If I were to pick out what I would do differently next time, I think I would take even more time to truly understand the user and refine my features. I included in my app a 'recipe magic' feature which I thought would be a unique feature to include in the app for families. Although I still believe the idea was good, I wish I had spent more time refining the feature and making it easier to use. Looking back it seems a bit plain and could have used a more thought out design considering it is a unique feature to the app.

"The most amazing part to me of this whole process was being able to create something out of nothing. Being able to combine creativity with research to solve problems was such a rewarding process and I am very proud of the final product."
